## The Central Design Concept: Die Gelbe Wand / The 34° Angle

Everything in the BVB visual system flows from a single architectural fact: the south standing terrace at Signal Iduna Park — *Die Gelbe Wand* ("The Yellow Wall") — sits at a **34-degree angle**. This is the largest standing section in any European football ground (~25,000 fans), and it creates the iconic sea of yellow on matchday.

**The 34° angle is not decorative. It IS the system:**

- It is cut into individual letterforms as the "Intensity Characters"
- It defines the diagonal graphic device used across all layouts
- It frames photography (refraction treatments)
- It drives motion design transitions and stadium LED animations
- It defines the wordmark construction geometry
- It appears in the custom jersey number system

If you use one design element that says "BVB", use this angle.

## Color Palette

### Primary Colors

| Name | Hex | Pantone | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| BVB Yellow | `#FDE100` | PMS Yellow 012 U | Dominant; backgrounds, text on black, fills |
| BVB Black | `#000000` | — | Secondary dominant; text, backgrounds |

**These two colors are non-negotiable.** They are the entire identity when used alone. Never dilute or replace them on primary touchpoints.

### Extended Palette (2023 Rebrand)

The extended palette is inspired by the stadium floodlights, vintage BVB kits (particularly 1990s neon periods), and Dortmund's industrial cityscape. Use for secondary content, accent moments, and digital-first work.

| Name | Hex (approx.) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Neon Yellow | `#E8FF00` | Accent; vintage kit reference; digital energy |
| Warm Grey | `#8C8C8C` | Industrial Dortmund; neutral carrier |
| Dark Grey | `#3A3A3A` | Alternative to full black for softer layouts |
| Off White / Concrete | `#F0EDE8` | Texture base; stadium concrete reference |

> ⚠️ The exact extended palette hex values are proprietary and not publicly released. The values above are close approximations. Always confirm from the official Frontify brand portal if you gain access.

### Color Rules

- **Primary layouts:** Yellow on Black, or Black on Yellow. Full stop.
- **Extended palette** is for secondary graphics, social content, experiential, and apparel details — never replaces the primary pair on hero assets.
- Neon yellow can appear alongside BVB Yellow for vibrancy but should not dominate.
- Avoid gradients between yellow and black. The contrast is intentional and harsh.

## Typography

### BVB Classic (Display / Headline)

- **Base:** Custom version of **Surt Extended** by Blaze Type
- **Key feature:** "Intensity Characters" — specific letterforms (notably crossbars and diagonals) feature deliberate cuts at the **34° angle**, breaking the letterform to inject energy and dynamism
- **Character:** Harsh, angular, industrial — compressed width, high x-height, aggressive presence
- **Usage:** Headlines, wordmark, campaign copy, score displays, player names (jersey numbers system)
- **Tone:** This typeface does not whisper. Every word set in it should feel like a stadium chant.

### Body / Functional Type

- A complementary, more neutral sans-serif balances the aggressive headline face
- Used for: editorial body copy, captions, UI text, labels, match programmes
- Must feel clean and recede behind the headline type — it is a workhorse, not a personality

### Number System

- Custom-designed to match the graphic device horizontals and diagonals
- The primary version (used on scoreboards, squad lists, social) is legible and bold
- A secondary "abstract" version exists for expressive/decorative use — use sparingly

### Typography Rules

- Headlines should be **uppercase** and **tight-tracked** (minimal letter spacing)
- Never mix BVB Classic with decorative typefaces — it IS the decoration
- Body type should sit in generous white (or black) space; BVB Classic needs room to breathe around it
- Avoid thin weights on black backgrounds at small sizes

## Graphic Device: The BVB Device / Yellow Wall Shape

The core graphic element is a **diagonal parallelogram/wedge shape** derived from the 34° angle of the terrace. It appears as:

- A **framing device** — cropping images, containing text
- A **dividing line** — splitting yellow and black zones at 34°
- A **motion transition** — wiping between states in video/animation
- A **texture overlay** — the diagonal is used in hatching and crosshatch patterns
- A **refraction filter** — photography is sometimes split or refracted through the device angle, creating an "intensity" effect on hero images

This device is the visual equivalent of a wipe of yellow across the pitch — energetic, directional, inevitable.

---

## Textures

The industrial landscape of Dortmund (steel works, mining, brutalist architecture) informs a suite of **surface textures**:

- Hatching and crosshatch patterns at 34°
- Gritty, grainy overlay effects (reminiscent of old print, stadium concrete)
- Worn/embossed yellow — as if printed on material
- Metal/steel surface textures for 3D logo treatments

Textures appear in:
- Background fills (not blocking yellow/black, but enriching them)
- Merchandise and packaging
- Experiential spaces (stadium tunnel, fan shop, museum)
- 3D logo renders

## Photography & Art Direction

Photography operates across **three levels** of intensity — this is a core part of the system:

| Level | Description | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| **1 — Everyday** | Candid, documentary, warm; fans and community moments | Editorial, social (lifestyle posts), community content |
| **2 — Intensity** | High-contrast, dynamic; match action, raw emotion, sweat and grit | Match day content, campaign hero imagery |
| **3 — Expressive** | Photography refracted, split, or overlaid through the 34° device; abstract and iconic | Campaign posters, broadcast openers, hero visuals |

**Photo treatment notes:**
- Level 1 photos often have a slightly hazy, candid quality — "fresh" and human
- Level 2 is high-contrast, often desaturated or with yellow color grading
- Level 3 uses the 34° graphic device as an actual lens/prism — images fragment or split at the angle
- Player portraits: restrained, direct, shot on black or grey backgrounds with the device used in framing
- Avoid stock-feeling imagery; authenticity is essential to *Echte Liebe*

## Logo & Crest

- The **crest (badge) is unchanged** from the pre-rebrand version — a classic shield with BVB 09 — and should never be modified, tilted, or distorted
- The **wordmark** ("BORUSSIA DORTMUND" in BVB Classic) was redesigned using the custom type
- The wordmark features the 34° cuts within specific letterforms
- A **variable/active logomark** concept exists — the crest can be used with motion treatments in broadcast (pulsing, texture-over animations) without altering the static badge form
- **BVB** abbreviation in BVB Classic is a secondary lockup used where the full name is unnecessary

### Logo Rules
- Always protect the crest with adequate clear space — minimum equivalent to the height of the "B" in BVB around all sides
- On yellow: use black crest
- On black: use yellow crest
- Never use the crest on any non-brand color without explicit approval
- Never place the crest inside the 34° graphic device — the device supports the logo, it doesn't contain it

## Motion & Digital

- All transitions reference the **34° angle** — wipes, reveals, slides
- The brand has been specifically designed for **agile multi-format deployment**: works on stadium LED boards (ultra-wide format), social stories (vertical), broadcast overlays, website
- **Stadium LED bands** are a key format — the design must work in extreme horizontal aspect ratios
- Motion should feel **kinetic but controlled** — not chaotic; the 34° is always the direction of energy

## Must-Have Visual References

These are the assets you **must study and have access to** before designing anything for BVB:

1. **The BVB crest / badge** — official vector file; never recreate from scratch
2. **BVB Classic typeface** — the custom Surt Extended variant; not a public font; requires license/access via BVB
3. **The 34° graphic device** — the diagonal shape template in vector format
4. **The "Intensity Characters" type specimen** — shows which specific glyphs have the 34° cuts
5. **The extended color palette swatch file** — official values from Frontify, not third-party approximations
6. **Photography examples at all three levels** — especially Level 3 refraction treatments
7. **The custom number system** — full numeral set, both legible and expressive versions
8. **Motion principles reference video** — how 34° transitions work in animation
9. **Stadium LED format templates** — extreme aspect ratio layouts
10. **The Frontify brand portal** — BVB's central hub; this is where guidelines, approved templates, and approved assets live. If you become a partner, access is granted here.

## Things That Will Get Your Work Rejected

- Using a generic yellow (e.g., `#FFCC00`) instead of BVB Yellow (`#FDE100`)
- Placing the crest on non-brand colors
- Using the 34° device at a different angle (even 33° or 35° is wrong)
- Typography that is not BVB Classic for headlines
- Photography that feels stock, cold, or generic
- Mixing the crest into the diagonal device
- Copy that sounds corporate or distanced from the fanbase
- Ignoring the Frontify portal and working from memory or web scrapes
